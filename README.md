# Commons Preservation License (CPL) v1.0

A license for sharing useful work freely while preventing its enclosure
behind proprietary or commercial restrictions.

---

## What is CPL?

CPL is a share-alike, non-commercial license aimed at preserving a durable
commons of code, data, and models.

It allows others to:

- Use the work
- Study it
- Modify it
- Redistribute it
- Train AI models on it

It requires that:

- Derivative works are also licensed under CPL
- Source (or an equivalent modifiable form) remains publicly available
- The work and derivatives are not used for commercial purposes
- The work and derivatives are not enclosed behind proprietary or paywalled access
- Models trained on CPL-covered work remain openly available, including weights
  and essential training details

The intent is to enable voluntary cooperation and open improvement, while making
it difficult to capture shared work into closed, proprietary systems.

---

## Applying CPL to your project

1. Add the CPL text to your project as `LICENSE`
   (or `LICENSE.txt` / `LICENSE.md`).
2. Replace the placeholders in the license:
   - `[YEAR]` → current year
   - `[AUTHOR]` → your name, handle, or organization
3. Whenever you distribute the project (source archive, binary, dataset,
   model weights, etc.), include the `LICENSE` file in that distribution.

No registration or central authority is involved; the license applies wherever
the work goes, as long as the text is included.

---

## Building on CPL-licensed work

If you create and distribute a derivative of CPL-licensed work:

- License your derivative under **CPL v1.0** as well.
- Include the CPL `LICENSE` file with your distribution.
- Keep the source (or equivalent modifiable form) **publicly available**, not
  only to direct recipients.
- Do not use the original or the derivative commercially.
- Do not place the original or derivative behind proprietary or paywalled access.
- If you train AI models on CPL-covered work, release the model and essential
  training details under terms compatible with CPL and keep them openly
  accessible.

Recommended notice for derivatives:

```md
This project is based on work licensed under the Commons Preservation License (CPL) v1.0.
This project is also licensed under CPL v1.0. See the LICENSE file for details.
```

## Purpose

CPL is for authors who want their work to remain part of a free commons:
usable, modifiable, and shareable by anyone, but resistant to being locked
away behind paywalls or proprietary terms.
